<h2>PaperTrail</h2>
<p></p>PaperTrail is a powerful, local‑first project and task tracker built for people who plan in weeks and months, and desire a low friction way to stay organized. Spend time up front to input your main projects and recurring tasks, let PaperTrail help with the rest. The default color palate is modeled after my favorite VS Code color theme, Solarized Dark. When you use this app, the goal is to reduce strain-- visually and mentally.</p>

The Vision:<br>
-clarity, reflection, and insight<br>
-persistence over pressure, so you can build habits<br>
-structure without rigidity<br>
<p>This is a desktop-only application that prioritizes clarity and insight, for people who like to see reflective patterns without the pressure of KPI driven metrics and gamification of modern software. I wanted a lofi environment that was easy on the eyes, free of visual clutter, and deceivingly powerful despite the low-tech feel. I built this app in a way that avoids the pressure of performance. It's simply designed to show me how I'm <i>already</i> performing. Anything can be a to-do list-- it's what your to-do list shows you that will make you want to come back over and over again. PaperTrail will help with habit building in a way that doesn't feel forced, because it is built to adapt to your thought patterns and display information in a way that feels natural. No more endless scrolling, no more jotting on random notepads, no more phone-note-graveyards. Just one app, designed to make large batches of information appear digestable, so you're taking in what you can handle without the visual overwhelm of an endless list.</p>

PaperTrail is meant to feel like a well‑used notebook.<br>
<p>Open it up, jot down thoughts and tasks, let your mind relax. You're not going to forget, because you've left yourself a PaperTrail that's always there when you need it.
Like a journal, post-its on the mirror, a grocery list on the fridge-- PaperTrail functions as a place to record the important things so you can come back to review your tasks, reflect on your week, and know you won't forget anything.</p>

The Why:<br>
Most task managers are bloated and built for speed, engagement, or urgency. Rather than help the user, they create stress and breadcrumb you with just enough dopamine that you keep coming back until you eventually burn out-- but not before you've paid a subscription fee, given them your data, and/or fed the capitalist machine. <br>
<b>PaperTrail is different.</b> Its design minimizes overwhelm and encourages follow‑through, consistency, and reflection. After you download it, you don't even need an internet connection. No accounts, no data mining, just a tool working the way a tool should. It's free. I don't request anything from you, and I'll never know who you are. If you're tired of being connected, this is for you. <br>

The Structure:<br>
-Supports ongoing work that repeats weekly or monthly<br>
-Tracks projects that need notes and documentation<br>
-Compiles weekly reviews to remind you of your progress<br>
-Exists as a minimal interface that stays out of the way<br>

The Benefit:<br>
-Object permanence. Clear organizational structure. See what you're doing well, stay ahead of your to-do list, keep an ongoing summary of what you've done.<br>
-Your PaperTrail will help answer the question: "What did I actually accomplish this week?"<br>

<h2>Core Features</h2>

<b>Tasks</b>

One‑off and recurring tasks
Inbox to quickly add uncategorized work
Projects with custom colors
Priority, due dates, and status tracking
Bulk actions for efficient cleanup

<b>Work‑Week Planning</b>

Calendar‑based planning for the current work week
Drag‑and‑drop task scheduling
Priority‑colored task indicators
Calendar remains visible even when lists are filtered

<b>Recurring Tasks & Monthly Bundles</b>
PaperTrail is especially well‑suited for recurring work.

Daily, weekly, biweekly, and monthly recurrence
Monthly bundle mode:

-Define multiple checklist items per month
-Check or uncheck items directly
-Optional notes when needed (Shift‑click)
-Completion history tracked per month

Bundle details stay open while you work

<b>Notes</b>

-Notes can stand alone or belong to a project
-Inline editing directly in the list
-Notes surface automatically during Weekly Review

<b>Weekly Review</b>

Dedicated Weekly Review view
Aggregates:

-Completed one‑off tasks
-Recurring task history
-Notes created during the week


-Easily copy Weekly Review output for status updates or your own reflection
-Optional CSV export


<b>Dashboard & Insights</b>
The dashboard provides a high‑level overview without pressure:

-Due Today, This Week, Overdue, and Recurring summaries
-Productivity heatmap for recent activity
-Trend charts for productivity, sleep, and mood

PaperTrail also generates simple written observations (e.g. relationships between sleep and productivity) to support reflection rather than raw analysis.

<b>Interface Philosophy</b>

-Clean task cards with actions placed deliberately
-Expand/collapse controls using consistent caret patterns
-Inline meta chips that wrap naturally
-Minimal horizontal scrolling
-Light, Dark, and SolarizedDark themes
-Focus mode collapses the sidebar for distraction‑free planning


<b>Keyboard Friendly</b>

Quick task entry
Inline editing
Escape to dismiss editors
Shift‑click for optional notes on bundle items


🔐 <b>Privacy & Storage</b><br>
<b>PaperTrail is local‑first by design.</b>

-All data is stored locally using IndexedDB
-No accounts required
-No telemetry or analytics
-Full export and import via plain .json files

<b>You own your data.</b> I will only see download metrics from GitHub. PaperTrail collects nothing from users and does not require an internet connection once downloaded. 

<b>Getting Started</b><br>
Local / Web

Open index.html in a modern browser
(or serve via a local static server for best results)

Desktop (Electron)

Launch the packaged app PaperTrail.exe, no installation required OR download PaperTrail-1.0.0 Setup.exe to install PaperTrail to your computer
<b>All data remains local to your machine</b>


<b>Backups</b>

Use Export Backup from Settings
Import restores projects, tasks, notes, and settings

Backups are human‑readable .json files.
